# TESS Program G011230
            
Title: The TESS Open Cluster Study: Planetary Evolution And The Impact Of The Stellar Environment

PI: Meibom, Soren - Harvard-Smithsonian Center For Astrophysics

Type: N/A

Summary: The search for transiting planets in clusters has been unsuccessful from the ground, and slow from space due to the limited sky-coverage of current space telescopes. With TESS, an all-sky mission, the TESS Open Cluster Survey will more than double the current number of cluster planets in Year-1 alone. Importantly, each TOCS planet will have well known physical properties, including age, making it a benchmark system placing important empirical constraints on models of the formation, evolution, and long-term survival of planets, planetary systems, and planetary atmospheres. An expanded population of cluster planets will also provide much needed guidance to N-body modelers seeking to better understand the dynamical evolution of planetary systems in potentially hostile cluster environments.
